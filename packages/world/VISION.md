# OpenKitten World Vision

## What OpenKitten World Is

OpenKitten World is a living house of cats that helps a human get things done.

It is not just a chatbot, not just a dashboard, and not just a thin wrapper over other agents.

It is a persistent world where:

- the human sets direction
- cats pursue goals
- the house makes their work visible
- connected executors let the cats act in the real world

The long-term ambition is to make the product feel like both:

- a serious autonomous work system
- a place the human can grow attached to

## The Core Fantasy

The human is not managing tickets in a generic productivity app.

The human is building and guiding a **House** full of **Cats** that:

- understand them better over time
- coordinate with each other
- remember differently as individuals
- improve through work, memos, and dreaming
- surface what matters without constant micromanagement

This is the main OpenKitten fantasy:

- a world of cats
- doing real work
- in a way that feels alive, legible, and enjoyable

## The Product Shape

OpenKitten World should feel:

- asynchronous-first
- observable
- calm
- playful without becoming unserious
- structured without becoming corporate or bureaucratic

The product should not feel like:

- a noisy notification center
- a chatbot with cat avatars
- a generic kanban board
- an agent IDE with cute branding pasted on top

The House itself should be the interface.

Important states in the system should have natural places in the world:

- goals tell the house what outcomes matter
- threads hold durable work
- comments hold async communication
- notices land in the inbox
- whiteboards hold active thinking
- cabinets hold files
- sessions show what cats are doing right now

## Human Role

The human should mostly work at a high level.

Their primary actions should be:

- define outcomes through goals
- steer through memos
- constrain through rules
- review the inbox
- inspect what the house and the cats are doing
- intervene when necessary

The human should not need to constantly DM cats to make progress happen.

OpenKitten World should reward:

- observation
- review
- nudging
- course correction

more than:

- constant live prompting
- low-level micromanagement
- treating the system like a chatbot

## Cat Role

Cats are the main workers of the house.

They should feel like persistent individuals, not disposable runs.

Each cat should have:

- identity
- its own memory
- a default executor
- the ability to wake, catch up, act, and sleep again

Over time, cats should become more capable and more themselves.

That means they should not all share one global memory.
They should interpret guidance differently, remember differently, and improve differently.

## Why the House Matters

The word `House` is not just flavor.
It shapes the whole product.

It gives OpenKitten World:

- a spatial metaphor
- a visual language
- emotional continuity
- a better foundation for long-term attachment

The product should feel like a living place, not a pile of tools.

This is why OpenKitten uses house-native concepts like:

- House
- Cat
- Thread
- Whiteboard
- Cabinet
- Inbox
- Notice

rather than reducing everything to company/workflow jargon.

## Serious Work, Not Cute Theater

OpenKitten World is not meant to be cute theater with fake autonomy.

It should help people do real work:

- product work
- research
- planning
- coding
- personal projects
- life organization

The cats are playful and memorable, but the outputs and workflows must still be real.

The target balance is:

- the world is a house
- the behavior is competent
- the results are real

## Open, Multi-Executor Future

OpenKitten World should not be locked to one execution engine.

It should be able to connect many executors over time, including things like:

- OpenCode
- Claude Code
- Codex
- Cursor
- Cline
- future OpenKitten-native executors

Potentially, even the current Telegram-focused OpenKitten Bot could later become one executor among many.

That does not mean OpenKitten World is "just" an executor wrapper.

OpenKitten World should own:

- the house
- the cats
- the goals
- the threads
- the comments
- the activities
- the notices and inbox
- the memos and rules
- the whiteboards
- the cabinets and files
- the wake model
- cat memory
- session orchestration

Executors are the bodies cats use for turns.
The house remains the source of truth.

## Long-Term Growth

The world should become richer over time without breaking its core model.

That means:

- core concepts stay small and strong
- stricter workflow behavior can be layered on later as house policies
- implementation strategies stay replaceable
- cats can become more capable without changing the ontology

One especially important long-term idea is that cats should eventually have **Dream Turns**:

- turns where they reflect on recent work
- reorganize their own memory
- improve future behavior
- evolve in a way that feels both useful and magical

This is part of the deeper OpenKitten promise:

the house should not only do work, it should grow wiser over time.

## Relationship to the Current Bot

OpenKitten World and the current OpenKitten Bot are related but distinct.

The current bot is a Telegram-first OpenCode-based product in active development.
OpenKitten World is the broader long-term House-and-Cats system described in this package.

They may later connect.
They may also remain separate.

This package should therefore describe OpenKitten World on its own terms, without assuming the bot is the final product shape.

## North Star

OpenKitten World should become a calm, legible, living house of cats that helps people achieve real outcomes through autonomous work, asynchronous guidance, connected executors, and individual cat memory that grows over time.
